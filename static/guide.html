Seattle's Food Scene

Seattle's culinary landscape is as diverse as its neighborhoods. From fresh Pacific seafood to international flavors, the Emerald City offers food lovers an incredible journey. Whether you're craving authentic Mediterranean, Asian fusion, or classic Pacific Northwest cuisine, Seattle delivers bold flavors and local passion in every bite.

My Personal Favorites

These are the spots I keep coming back to - the places that feel like home.

Aladdin's Gyro-cery

Authentic Mediterranean flavors that never disappoint. This local gem serves up some of the best gyros and shawarma in the city.

What to Order:

  • Spicy Chicken Aladdin Fries - Get extra sauce, trust me
  • Chicken Shawarma - Perfectly seasoned and grilled to perfection

Sizzle and Crunch

A local favorite for customizable rice bowls packed with flavor and fresh ingredients.

What to Order:

  • Half Rice, Half Steak Rice Bowl - Load it up with all the veggies for the perfect balance

Popular Food Spots by Neighborhood

Click each neighborhood to discover the best eats in the area.

Pike Place Market & Downtown

The heart of Seattle's food scene with fresh seafood, artisan vendors, and iconic eateries.

Pike Place Chowder

Award-winning clam chowder and seafood bisques. Try the New England Clam Chowder - it's won national awards.

Piroshky Piroshky

Russian pastries baked fresh throughout the day. The smoked salmon pâté and beef & cheese piroshkies are must-tries.

Beecher's Handmade Cheese

Watch cheese being made through the window, then try their famous mac and cheese - it's worth the line.

Capitol Hill

Seattle's eclectic neighborhood known for diverse dining, vibrant nightlife, and creative cuisine.

Paseo

Caribbean-inspired sandwiches that have achieved cult status. The Caribbean Roast sandwich is messy, massive, and absolutely delicious.

Din Tai Fung

World-renowned Taiwanese restaurant famous for xiao long bao (soup dumplings). The pork dumplings are perfection.

Molly Moon's Homemade Ice Cream

Local ice cream shop using organic, local ingredients. Seasonal flavors rotate, but the salted caramel is a staple favorite.

Ballard

A historic Scandinavian neighborhood turned foodie haven with craft breweries and innovative restaurants.

The Walrus and the Carpenter

Oyster bar serving some of the freshest seafood in Seattle. Arrive early or expect a wait - no reservations.

Un Bien

Caribbean street food served from a vintage Airstream trailer. The roasted corn and sandwiches are phenomenal.

Stoneburner

Italian-inspired cuisine with wood-fired pizzas and house-made pastas in a modern, airy space.
